## Comparative Molecular Docking of fluoroquinolone Antibiotics Agaainst E.coli DNA Gyrase B
**Objective:** To compare selected fluoroquinolones based on: 

               1. Molecular docking scores
               2. Predicted binding pocket 
               3. drug- likeness properties
               4. Pharmacokinetic suitability
               
**Target protein:**

               1. Ciprofloxacin
               2. Norpfloxacin
               3. Levofloxacin
               
**Methods:** 
              1.**Molecular docking:**
              
                     1. Platform: CB-Dock2
                     2. Scoring Engine: AutoDock Vina
                     3. Blind docking with cavity detection
              2.**ADME Analysis:**
              
                     1. Platform: SwissADME
                     
                     2. Parameters evaluated:
                     
                             1. Molecular weight
                             2. LogP
                             3. GI absorption
                             4. Lipinski rule of five
                             5. Solubilty
                             
**Key findings:**

                1. Ciprofloxacin showed strongest predicted binding with an dock score of -9.3 compared to Norfloxacin with an dock score of -9.2 and Levofloxacin an dock score of -8.3
                2. All three compounds had high GI absorption
                3. All satisfied lipinski rule of five
                4. Ciprofloxacin ranked best overall

**Conclusion:** Among these tested fluoroquinolones, Ciprofloxacin emerged as the best overall candidate based on combined docking and ADME profiling against E.coli DNA gyrase B. This study demonstrates the use of computational screening tools for a rapid antibiotic comparison.
